My Time in Ireland


At Home

I am the eldest between me and my sister to my Irish mom and black dad.



School

I went to Clonaghadoo.



Gaming

While me and my sister were sitting way too close to the TV watching cartoons, my dad came home from work with a bright yellow box that turned out to be a PlayStation 2 he won at work. He had also got a Tiger Woods’ golf game. We soon had it set up and me, my sister, and Dad were playing golf, passing the controller between each other. We later got a few more games and I bought a memory card. I could finally save my progress in games. 2007-2008



Drawing

While I was at Mountmelik and Clonaghadoo, my middle school and elementary schools in Ireland, I would always draw and sketch. I had my first official drawing class in Mountmelik which I wasn’t great at, but would draw often.



Sports

While at a friend’s house opposite the local football field, I heard a lot of noise coming from over the hedge. I asked my friend’s mom what was going on and she brought me across to see. I saw a bunch of my friends playing football with each other and I asked if I could go play. She said sure and so I went over, was given a jersey and played with my friends. I just had fun kicking the ball around. This was my first introduction to Gaelic Football, a sport that I ended up sticking with.



Moving to America

So in 2013, my family had made plans to go for a summer-long vacation to America. And at that time there was no underlying issue or experience behind it. It was just for my sister and I, and it was just going to be a trip. For the first few weeks we were just bouncing around locations, but we spent the majority of the time in California with cousins. We had so much fun and were enjoying our break. Then, near the end, in our last week of the vacation, Mom and Dad brought my sister and I to a restaurant in the town that we were living in during the summer. San Luis Obispo. They brought us there and sat down opposite my sister and I to ask us how our vacation was, what our experience was like, and what we thought of it all. And then my mom asked, “What would you think of the idea of moving here?” To which Ella, my sister, got very excited, very quickly about the amazing amount of things to do and opportunities for her. I was very shy about it because I remember saying that I was nervous of losing my friends in Ireland. And during that night at the restaurant and the following day, we talked a bit more about it. And then, I guess, I agreed to moving to the States. So, after the US trip, we went back home and packed up our entire house that I spent my entire life in. We packed it up entirely in two weeks. Then, on October 14th, we were now living in the States. And the next day, my sister and I started middle school.
